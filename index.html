Interests

Look for the fundamental mechanism in complex system

Coupling in Cardiac myocite system

Starting from the observation on cardiac cell culture, we build a mathematical model to study the fundamental mechanism of nonlinear behavior in biological system.

Quroum sensing

Having consensus from a population is important in social network. We study the mechanism of decision making in bacteria world through mathematical model and investigate complex behavior from this small organism.

Public bike sharing system

By analyzing the data from public bike sharing system in Boston, we study the user behavior during summer and the problematic station resulting to outage of the system through mode localization.

Heatshock response

Self-rescue could be fatal if the response is at the wrong timing! We study the stress-protein regulation in C. elegance and found different regulation network structures can result in various rescue efficiencies in response to environmental stress.

Proofreading mechanism in dDNA

Speed, Stability and Stringency are the key features for successful downstream regulation during DNA paring. We show different strategies for paring of two rod-like DNA model and provide insight of synthesis self-essembly.

Interactions between nucleus in the brain

Multi-time scale regulation is the most potential biomarker of noninvasive disease diagnosis. By identifying the role of nucleus in the brain, the possible control mechanism is investigated.

About Me

My post graduate study focused on the nonlinear dynamics in biological complex systems and had found the fundamental mechanism of the phenomena that we observed in experiments. By utilizing numerical simulations and theoretical approach, biological mechanisms were revealed through modeling studies and brought up two publications in 2011, one in 2013. The first work is based on the experimental observation of cardiac myocyte cultures. Our results suggested that the beating rate variation is due to the changes of coupling strength between the cells. The second paper is about “Quorum Sensing”. In this work, we provided a simple model to mimic the system and try to find the fundamental mechanism of this collective behavior by numerical simulation and stability analysis. In my first postdoctoral training, I was interested in constructing the models that can reproduce and explain the phenomena we observed empirically. The origin and mechanism of fractal structure in the signal of Suprachiasmatic nucleus was investigated. Based on the understanding of the characteristics of the signals, we constructed models to mimic the empirical signals and find the potential mechanism caused by aging and disease. Signal analyzes results in one paper published in June 2015 and May 2016.To continue my interests in investigating the fundamental mechanism in complex biological system, I switch to department of physics, try to use numerical simulation to explain the phenomena we observed in pathogenic infection system and look for the most important parameter. In this study, we focus on how the fluid velocity of mucus affects the pathogen colonization on the intestine in the model system of C.elegans. We find there is an optimal fluid velocity for colonization growth because of the tradeoff between pathogen influx and detachment due to the mucus fluid. In further, by estimating the virulent factor that pathogen had secreted and introduce the tolerance to this virulent factor, we estimate the life span of C.elegans under different feeding condition and successfully explain the results shown in 2000 that the life span is nonlinearly depend on the pathogenic feeding duration. Beyond this project, I am also looking at how heat-shock response propagate through the whole body of C.elegans via local(tissuetissue interaction) and global(neuron-tissue interaction) connection when the local heat stress is applied. Beside these simulation project, I am also working on the mechanism of dDNA recognition process purely from the analytical point of view (collaborate with Prof. Mara Prentiss's group). In this project, we find the generic strategy of balancing among accuracy(specificity), speed, redundancy, mortality and energy expenditure in the non-equilibrium biological system and published in March 2017. Because of pervious experience on neuron science in sleep medicine, I understand how important it is to realize the real neuron connection in the brain when constructing a mathematical model. With this neural network, I can build a more realistic model base on the empirical observation and utilize this model to understand the relation between brain structure and function, therefore find out the possible mechanism for brain disease.
